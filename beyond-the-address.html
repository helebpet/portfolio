Beyond the Address

Fall 2024

Editorial Design

Concept

Beyond the Address is a magazine that explores the significance of house numbers in the Presidio Heights neighborhood of San Francisco. The magazine investigates the history of house numbers, the development of the neighborhood, and the ways in which these small typographic elements contribute to the larger identity of a community. Through design research, the project reveals how often overlooked house numbers help define the character of a place and landscape.

Design Approach

The magazine's design reflects the modern, minimal aesthetic of Presidio Heights, where many house numbers are influenced by contemporary trends. The typeface Futura was chosen because it closely resembles Neutraface, a commercial typeface commonly used for house numbers in the neighborhood. This design decision ties the project to the local architectural identity while maintaining a clean, modern visual language.

Production

The project was created in Adobe InDesign, with photographs captured using a digital camera and edited for the final layout. Motivated by the theme of typography in the community, the project focuses on how typographic elements (like house numbers) play an understated yet vital role in shaping a neighborhood's identity. By highlighting these often overlooked design elements, Beyond the Address pays homage to the unique house numbers that contribute to the distinctiveness of Presidio Heights.
